What Are Card Skimmers?

Card skimmers are illegal devices criminals attach to legitimate card readers to steal your card information when you swipe or insert your card.

These malicious devices are typically placed on ATMs, gas pumps, and other point-of-sale terminals. They're designed to blend in and look like part of the original machine, making them difficult to spot without careful inspection.

Normal vs. Compromised Card Readers

Normal Card Reader

  • Flush with the machine surface
  • Consistent color and material
  • No loose parts or components
  • Keypad feels solid and normal
  • Card slot is secure and well-fitted

Compromised Card Reader

  • Bulky or protruding card slot
  • Different color or material than rest of machine
  • Loose or wiggly components
  • Keypad overlay feels different or soft
  • Extra cameras or unusual devices nearby

Common Types of Skimming Devices

Overlay Skimmers

These fit over the existing card slot and read your card as it passes through to the real reader.

Warning Signs:

  • Card slot appears bulkier than normal
  • Slot wiggles or moves when touched
  • Different color or material than the rest of the machine

Deep-Insert Skimmers

These are inserted deep into the card reader slot and are very difficult to detect visually.

Warning Signs:

  • Card doesn't insert smoothly
  • Unusual resistance when inserting card
  • Card feels "gripped" or sticky when inserted

Keypad Overlays

These are placed over the keypad to capture your PIN while a separate device captures your card data.

Warning Signs:

  • Keypad feels spongier than usual
  • Keys are harder to press or feel different
  • Keypad appears thicker or raised

Real-World Examples

Common Locations

  • ATMs - especially in tourist areas or isolated locations
  • Gas Pumps - pumps farthest from the store are common targets
  • Retail POS Terminals - particularly in busy stores with limited supervision
  • Transit Ticket Machines - often in crowded stations

Warning Signs

  • Card slot feels loose or wiggles when touched
  • Parts of the machine look newer or a different color
  • Unusual attachments or wires visible
  • Security seals on gas pumps are broken or tampered with
  • Hidden cameras pointing at the keypad

How to Protect Yourself

Before Using a Card Reader

  • Inspect the card reader for any signs of tampering
  • Wiggle the card slot to check if it's loose
  • Compare the machine to others nearby to spot differences
  • Look for broken seals or security stickers (especially on gas pumps)
  • Check for hidden cameras near the keypad
  • Use our CardGuardian app to scan the device

During and After Transactions

  • Always cover the keypad with your hand when entering PIN
  • Use contactless payment methods when available
  • Use credit cards instead of debit cards when possible
  • Monitor your accounts regularly for suspicious activity
  • Report suspicious devices to the establishment and authorities
  • If you suspect a skimmer, use a different machine
